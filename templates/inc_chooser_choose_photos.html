How does this work?

First, start by dragging the map around.

As you do, you should see the latitude and longitude appear (and change) beneath the map. Those coordinates tell you where the center of the map (the crosshairs) is. After a short delay, the map will try to reverse geocode those coordinates to tell you where it thinks that place is in human-speak.

Next, try the find my location and find this place buttons. The map will zoom automatically to that location and update the latitude and longitude accordingly.

Once you're confortable with the map, click on a photo thumbnail in the ribbon below the map. This will load a larger version in the space you reading this reading this text.

You can ask the map to try and guess where that photo was taken by analyzing its tags and the refine the location by panning and zooming the map.

Once you've positioned the cross-hairs in the correct spot on the map click the big pink suggest this location button. You will be asked to confirm your suggestion and then, once you do, it will be recorded and displayed to the photo owner the next time they visit the site.
